Journey into Early Computer Music
OlbosBoy 125 items 22 October, 2023
- - -

Digital techniques for sound processing and synthesis come from a field of research whose history is complex and fascinating. Long before digital synthesizers and personal computers came on the market and digital sounds became the usual domain of popular music, a vast network of composers, engineers and researchers conceived almost all of the techniques still used today. Not only is it important to remember their names for the technical contribution they brought, but it is fascinating to listen to their compositions because they manifest a futuristic and alternative vision to the dominant narrative of the time. We can consider the research in the field of computer music as a subset of the broader research in the field of electroacoustic music. In this list are contained only works obtained through digital technologies in the period before the spread of personal computers, when it was necessary to access the mainframe computers installed in industrial or academic research centers.The process of sound synthesis was not in real time and it was necessary to wait even twelve hours to obtain the result of the required calculation and often the instructions to be given to the computer were controlled by hundreds of punched cards. The computers were often divided between different faculties of the university, so these pioneers of digital sound worked through the night. This is how the history of computer music was made: sleepless nights lived with the enthusiasm and impatience to finally hear the result of the next experiment.

Digital Toypop :3
Aeralix 102 items 07 March, 2026
Not to be conflated with the genre Toypop, a folk-rooted experimental genre mostly in France and Japan in the 2000s. For a thorough and brilliant explanation on Toypop please check out the RYM Ultimate Box Set > Toypop.

What my list aims to do is to capture a newer wave of Toypop mostly self-assigned by (but not exclusive to) Doujin Music artists who take preexisting forms of Digital and Bit music like Bitpop, Digital Fusion, Chiptune, etc. as well as certain EDM genres like Kawaii Future Bass and apply a "toy" sonic palette.

Instruments such as the toy piano, higher range keyboard percussion (xylophone, glockenspiel), accordion, etc. as well as cartoonish sound effects like whistles, squeaks, slides, and others are very common. Album imagery typically features lots of colors and is cartoonish / cute by nature, using things like desserts (baked goods), toys / toy instruments, animals, etc.

This introduction and application of "toy pop" and its bright, cartoonish sound to (mostly) Doujin Music is more of an aesthetic, and as a result artists in this list have quite different sounds and subgenres they create under, but while maintaining a pretty identifiable sonic palette.



Tags that are searchable in this list:

#Bitpop #DF (Digital Fusion) #KFB (Kawaii Future Bass) #Akishibu-kei #Denpa #J-core #Lounge #EDM #Downtempo #ArtPop

The most relevant tags on a release are listed first :)

Proto-Glam! (1964 - 1971)
HTprods 21 items 14 March, 2026
A list of records that anticipate glam rock! This is short because I've tried to make it about records that truly sound the part. I make no claims that any of these were necessarily influential on the genre's development but I feel that all of them have glam attributes ahead of schedule. 1971 is the cut-off point because I think most people would agree that glam as a movement solidified in 1972. Though 1971 is quite late for "proto-glam", there are still relevant records from that year which have nothing to do with the larger story of the genre.

I have left out releases by The Velvet Underground - I don't think anyone needs me to tell them how important they were to glam rock. I've also left out any early material by Marc Bolan and David Bowie for similar reasons. If it's of any interest, I'd consider "King of the Rumbling Spires" the first glam rock record made by either Bolan or Bowie.

Any suggestions or comments welcome!

For a quick primer on glam rock proper, check out this 35-track playlist of big hits and dazzling obscurities I made titled GLAM ON!

Contemporary Library Music
aroliver 87 items 28 January, 2026
A list of "Library Music" made in the 21st century. I put Library Music in quotes because a lot of this music may not literally be made for commercial use or for hire, but shares a lot of the sonic characteristics of Library Music that was made for hire back in its heyday. As such, a lot of the music on this list can be seen as the artists' self-aware continuation and extension of the Library Music genre, as they often tag themselves as such, knowingly paying musical tribute to their predecessors. As such, I believe that this "new wave" of Library Music is an important part of the genre that gets overlooked.

Often, when one finds this new Library Music on Bandcamp or other such websites, it is tagged/described with genre terms like: "corporate training film music," "Corporate Music," "Production Music," and of course, Library Music itself, lending to the previously mentioned self aware replication of such musics that existed in the past, and making a case for their importance to the genre now.

In addition to albums that try to emulate the sounds of older Library Music albums, I will also include albums that are specifically made to be used in production by others, as long as the sounds aren't too different. This will be more literal Library or "Production" Music as the genre was originally.
